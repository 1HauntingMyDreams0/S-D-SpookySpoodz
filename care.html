Jumping Spider Care Guide

Everything you need to keep your spood healthy, happy, and thriving.

Housing

Jumpers love height, hiding spots, and airflow.

✔ Cross-ventilation
✔ Vertical climbing space
✔ Leaves, branches, or cork bark
✔ Secure lid (they are tiny escape artists)

Temperature

Keep between 70–80°F. Avoid drafts and extreme heat.

Humidity

Maintain around 60–80% humidity.
Light misting once daily is enough for hydration.

Feeding

Active hunters that need regular meals:

Slings: every 1–2 days
Juveniles: every 2–3 days
Adults: every 3–5 days

Feed fruit flies, bean beetles, or bottle flies.

Lighting

Provide natural or indirect light. Jumpers rely on vision to hunt.

Handling

Keep handling gentle and minimal. Let them walk onto you—never grab.

Molting

What is Molting?

Molting is when a spider sheds its exoskeleton in order to grow.

Signs of Molting

Refusing food, hiding in a web hammock, low activity, and dull coloration.

During the Molt

Do not disturb. Avoid feeding and keep conditions stable.

After Molting

Wait a few days before feeding while their body hardens.

Small spider, big personality

Jumping spiders are curious, intelligent, and incredibly interactive.

Consistency is key

Stable conditions and routine care lead to a healthy, thriving spider.

Jumping Spider Care FAQs

Providing the best care for your jumping spider is key to their longevity and happiness. Here are some of the most common questions we get from new owners.

Key Care Basics

Focus on three essentials:

✔ Temperature: 70–85°F
✔ Humidity: 70%+
✔ Arboreal enclosure with vertical space

Common Mistakes

The biggest mistake is using enclosures that are too short.

Jumping spiders need height to climb, explore, and feel secure.

Health Warning Signs

Watch for:

✔ Sudden appetite loss
✔ Low movement
✔ Pre-molt behavior
✔ Unusual body changes

If Something Seems Wrong

First, check temperature and humidity.

If conditions are correct, give your spider space and monitor closely.
